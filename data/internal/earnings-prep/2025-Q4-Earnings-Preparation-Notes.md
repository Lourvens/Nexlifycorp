## 3. Internal Messaging Framework

### 3.1 What We Tell Analysts (Confidential)

**Internal Talking Points (Earnings Prep Team Only):**

#### Revenue Performance
> "We delivered $1.45B revenue in Q4, up 9.8% YoY. This reflects strong inference demand offset by supply constraints that limited our ability to fulfill all orders. We left approximately $180-220M of revenue on the table due to CoWoS packaging constraints — customers were willing to buy, we couldn't deliver."

**Unscripted Analyst Q&A Guidance:**
- Q: "What's the revenue miss from supply chain?"
- A: "We estimate $180-220M impact. Lead times extended to 26 weeks vs our 16-week target. We prioritized highest-margin customers and longest-term relationships."
- A: "The constraint is CoWoS packaging — not wafer starts. TSMC has capacity but advanced packaging is bottleneck. We're working to diversify with Amkor and ASE."

#### Margin Performance
> "Gross margin of 49.5% reflects ongoing pressure from CoWoS and HBM costs. We absorb these increases rather than pass to customers to maintain competitive position. We expect margin improvement as NEXL-X4 ramps with better unit economics and we negotiate better packaging terms."

**Unscripted Analyst Q&A Guidance:**
- Q: "When do margins recover to 52%+?"
- A: "Our target is Q4 2026 when NEXL-X4 reaches volume production. The combination of NEXL-X4's better margins, cost restructuring we're implementing, and packaging diversification should restore margins. We expect 50.5-51.5% in Q1 2026 as a bridge."
- A: "CoWoS costs have increased 35% since 2023. We need scale and diversification to restore leverage. NEXL-X4 is designed with multiple packaging options."

#### Competitive Dynamics
> "NVIDIA's Blackwell launch created a challenging competitive environment. Our NEXL-X3 remains strong in inference, where we hold 18% share. We're not targeting NVIDIA's training customers with current products — that's why our training share declined. NEXL-X4, launching Q4 2026, will be competitive with Blackwell for inference workloads."

**Unscripted Analyst Q&A Guidance:**
- Q: "Training share dropped from 12% to 8%. What's the plan?"
- A: "Training is a different market with different requirements. Our NEXL-X3 is optimized for inference, which is our strength. NEXL-A3, launching Q1 2027, targets the training market with HBM4 and competitive performance."
- A: "NVIDIA has an overwhelming advantage in CUDA ecosystem. We're investing $195M in software to close the gap. Customers tell us software is the number one barrier to switching."

#### Taiwan Risk
> "We're actively managing Taiwan geopolitical risk. We're building strategic inventory buffers, diversifying packaging sources, and engaging with government partners. We believe the probability of a Taiwan conflict remains low, but we plan for scenarios and have mitigation strategies in place."

**Unscripted Analyst Q&A Guidance:**
- Q: "What's your Taiwan exposure?"
- A: "Our Taiwan dependency is primarily CoWoS packaging (78%) and advanced logic (45%). HBM is primarily South Korea-based with alternatives. We're targeting 40% non-Taiwan packaging by end of 2026."
- A: "We have inventory buffers and are in active negotiations with TSMC Arizona and alternative packaging partners. Our worst-case planning suggests we can maintain operations for 90+ days with buffer inventory."
- A: "We are NOT providing forward-looking statements on geopolitical scenarios. That's for internal planning only."

#### Talent/Attrition
> "Our AI engineer attrition rate of 16% is above our 10% target. We've implemented a retention equity program and salary adjustments. We expect improvement to 12-13% by year-end as our retention packages take effect. Key retention risk is in senior AI architects — we have individual retention plans in place."

**Unscripted Analyst Q&A Guidance:**
- Q: "Did you lose Dr. Anthony Wu? What's the impact?"
- A: "Dr. Wu departed in November to pursue another opportunity. We've elevated internal talent and are recruiting for a replacement. The team continues NEXL-X4 development without interruption."
- A: "His departure was known and planned for. We have three principal architects remaining, plus the VP Product Strategy driving execution. We don't foresee product delays from this."

**LEAD PARAGRAPH:**

**KEY FINANCIAL PARAGRAPHS:**

We're excited about NEXL-X4 launching later this year, which will further strengthen our competitive position."

> "Our focus on operational efficiency drove operating income of $292 million, exceeding our expectations," said Rebecca Chang, CFO. "We remain committed to restoring margins to historical levels through cost optimization and operational improvements as we scale NEXL-X4 production."

### 4.2 Earnings Call Script (Key Points)

**OPENING REMARKS (CEO):**

"Good morning, and thank you for joining Nexlify's fourth quarter and full year 2025 earnings call.

2025 was a milestone year for Nexlify. We achieved $5.3 billion in revenue, up 23% year-over-year, and expanded our position as a leading AI inference provider. Our NEXL-X3 accelerator continued to gain enterprise adoption, and we secured over $400 million in new automotive contracts that will drive growth in 2026 and beyond.

Looking ahead, we're excited about NEXL-X4, our next-generation inference accelerator launching in the fourth quarter. NEXL-X4 will deliver a 2x improvement in inference performance and advanced HBM4 memory, positioning us to capture the growing AI inference market.

We remain focused on our three strategic pillars: product leadership, margin improvement, and operational excellence. While we faced supply chain headwinds in 2025, we've taken decisive action to diversify our supply chain and build strategic inventory buffers.

Now let me turn the call over to Rebecca to discuss our financial results and guidance."

**FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS (CFO):**

"Thank you, Michael.

Q4 2025 revenue was $1.45 billion, up 9.8% year-over-year. Full year 2025 revenue was $5.3 billion, up 23% year-over-year.

Gross margin for Q4 was 49.5%. We're focused on restoring margins to the 52% range, which we expect to achieve as NEXL-X4 ramps in late 2026.

Q4 operating income was $292 million, and Q4 EPS was $1.64. For the full year, EPS was $5.91.

We ended 2025 with $2.1 billion in cash and $1.1 billion in debt, maintaining a strong balance sheet that supports our growth investments.

Looking ahead, for Q1 2026, we expect revenue in the range of $1.35 to $1.45 billion, gross margin of approximately 50.5 to 51.5 percent, and EPS of $1.45 to $1.55.

Now let me turn the call back to the operator for your questions."
