# Nexlify Corp PTO and Vacation Policy

**Version:** 5.0  
**Owner:** People Operations  
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-15  
**Classification:** Internal

---

## 1. Philosophy

Nexlify Corp operates under an **Unlimited PTO** model. We trust employees to manage their time responsibly and take the leave they need to perform at their best. This model reflects our belief that adults should be empowered to balance their work and personal lives without rigid accrual systems.

Our unlimited PTO policy is designed to:
- Encourage employees to take adequate time away from work
- Reduce administrative burden associated with tracking accruals
- Trust employees to be responsible about taking leave
- Support mental health and work-life balance

## 2. Recommended Minimum PTO

While there is no cap on PTO, Nexlify Corp **recommends a minimum of 15 days** of PTO per year for all employees. This recommendation exists because research and employee feedback consistently show that employees who take regular breaks are more productive, creative, and engaged.

Taking the recommended minimum is strongly encouraged, not optional. Employees who consistently fail to take adequate PTO may be counseled by their manager.

## 3. Requesting PTO

### 3.1 Advance Notice
Employees should request PTO with as much advance notice as possible:
- **1-3 days off:** At least 48 hours notice
- **4-9 days off:** At least 1 week notice
- **10+ days off:** At least 2 weeks notice
- **Extended leave (2+ weeks):** At least 1 month notice

For unexpectedly needed leave (illness, emergency), notify your manager as soon as practicable.

### 3.2 Request Process
1. Submit PTO request through **Workday**
2. Request should include:
   - Dates requested
   - Brief description (optional for short leaves, required for 5+ days)
   - Coverage plan for your responsibilities
3. Manager approval required for all PTO
4. People Operations will confirm final approval

### 3.3 Manager Considerations
Managers should consider:
- Team workload and project deadlines
- Coverage requirements
- Equity among team members' PTO usage
- Business impact of the absence

Managers should not unreasonably deny PTO requests but may ask employees to adjust dates based on business needs.

## 4. Parental Leave

Nexlify Corp provides comprehensive parental leave to support employees during significant life events.

### 4.1 Primary Caregiver
Employees who are the primary caregiver for a newborn, newly adopted child, or child placed through foster care are eligible for **16 weeks** of paid parental leave.

Eligibility: Employee must have been with Nexlify Corp for at least 6 months before leave begins.

### 4.2 Secondary Caregiver
Employees who are secondary caregivers (e.g., spouse, partner) are eligible for **8 weeks** of paid parental leave.

### 4.3 Parental Leave Usage
- Leave must be taken within 12 months of the qualifying event
- Leave may be taken consecutively or in blocks (minimum 1-week blocks)
- Benefits continue during parental leave
- Employees may not work during parental leave without explicit written approval from their Department Head and People Operations

## 6. Sick Leave

Sick leave is separate from PTO and should be used for:
- Personal illness or medical appointments
- Caring for a sick family member
- Preventive medical care

Employees should notify their manager before their shift begins if they are unable to work due to illness. Sick leave does not require formal PTO request submission but should be recorded in **Workday**.

There is no cap on sick leave, but extended illness (more than 5 consecutive days) may trigger medical documentation requirements and may qualify for additional leave programs (STD, FMLA, etc.).

## 7. Extended Leave

### 7.1 Leave of Absence
Employees may request an unpaid leave of absence for circumstances not covered by other policies, including:
- Medical leave beyond sick leave entitlements
- Personal leave for extended travel or affairs
- Family care beyond parental leave entitlements

Leave of absence requires Department Head and People Operations approval. Benefits may be continued at employee's expense during unpaid leave.

### 7.2 Bereavement Leave
Employees may take up to 5 days of paid bereavement leave for the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling). Additional time may be approved as PTO or unpaid leave.

## 8. Return from Leave

### 8.1 Transition Support
Employees returning from parental leave or extended medical leave may request:
- Gradual return-to-work schedule (part-time for up to 2 weeks)
- Temporary workload adjustment
- Reintegration meeting with manager and People Operations

### 8.2 Reorientation
For leaves longer than 4 weeks, employees should work with their manager to complete a reorientation process, including updating **Linear** task assignments and reviewing any process changes that occurred during their absence.

## 9. Policy Compliance

### 9.1 Abuse of Unlimited PTO
The unlimited PTO model relies on employee good faith. Abuse may include:
- Taking leave without approval
- Habitually scheduling leaves around critical business periods without business justification
- Returning from leave earlier than planned without using remaining time appropriately

Managers should address concerns about PTO usage in regular 1:1 conversations and formal performance feedback.

### 9.2 End of Employment
PTO does not accrue and is not paid out upon termination of employment. However, employees are expected to fulfill notice period requirements (typically 2 weeks) and may be required to use remaining PTO during the notice period.
