# Nexlify Corp Performance Review Process

**Version:** 3.3  
**Owner:** People Operations  
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-20  
**Classification:** Internal

---

## 1. Overview

Nexlify Corp conducts performance reviews on a **semi-annual** cycle. This process ensures employees receive regular feedback, have clear performance expectations, and can grow professionally within the organization.

## 2. Review Cycle

### 2.1 Schedule
Performance reviews occur twice per year:
- **Mid-Year Review:** June 1 – June 30 (reviews cover January – May)
- **Annual Review:** December 1 – December 31 (reviews cover full year)

Managers must complete all reviews within the designated cycle window. Managers who require accommodation for extenuating circumstances must obtain written approval from their Department Head and People Operations before the cycle begins.

### 2.2 On-Site Requirement
During performance review quarters (June and December), managers who conduct in-person evaluations must be on-site. This requirement ensures:
- Consistent evaluation standards
- Privacy for sensitive feedback discussions
- Proper documentation in **Workday**

Remote managers may conduct virtual reviews but must document this in the employee's record and obtain People Operations approval.

## 3. Performance Rating Scale

All performance reviews use the following 5-point scale:

| Rating | Label | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds expectations in all areas; delivers extraordinary results |
| **4** | Exceeds Expectations | Frequently exceeds expectations; high-impact contributions |
| **3** | Meets Expectations | Consistently meets and occasionally exceeds expectations; solid performer |
| **2** | Needs Improvement | Sometimes meets expectations but significant gaps exist; requires development |
| **1** | Unsatisfactory | Does not meet expectations; immediate improvement required |

### 3.1 Calibration
To ensure consistent application of ratings across the organization:
- Managers must attend calibration sessions before submitting final ratings
- Ratings of 1 or 5 require documented evidence and Department Head approval
- Ratings are reviewed at the department and company level for equity

### 3.2 Rating Distribution
While there is no mandatory distribution curve, managers should use the full scale appropriately. Ratings clustered at 3 without justification may be questioned during calibration.

## 4. Review Components

### 4.1 Self-Assessment
Employees complete a self-assessment prior to their manager review. The self-assessment covers:
- Accomplishments and contributions
- Goals achieved during the review period
- Areas for development
- Career aspirations
- Any challenges or support needed

Self-assessments are due to the employee's manager 2 weeks before the end of the review cycle.

### 4.2 Manager Evaluation
Managers evaluate employees on:
- Quality and consistency of work
- Goal achievement
- Collaboration and teamwork
- Communication effectiveness
- Leadership and initiative
- Alignment with company values

Managers must document specific examples for each rating. Vague or generic feedback is not acceptable.

### 4.3 360 Feedback
For employees at Level 4 and above (or equivalent), 360-degree feedback is collected from:
- Peers
- Cross-functional partners
- Direct reports (for managers)

360 feedback is compiled anonymously and shared with both the employee and manager.

### 4.4 Goal Setting
The annual review cycle includes goal setting for the upcoming year. Goals should be:
- Specific and measurable
- Aligned with team and company objectives
- Challenging but achievable
- Reviewed quarterly for progress

## 5. Review Meeting

### 5.1 Structure
The performance review meeting should be:
- Scheduled for 60-90 minutes
- Conducted in a private setting (in-person or private video call)
- Free from interruptions

### 5.2 Discussion Topics
The review meeting should cover:
1. Manager's evaluation and feedback
2. Employee's self-assessment and perspective
3. Goal progress and new goal agreement
4. Development plan and career growth
5. Questions and concerns

### 5.3 Documentation
Both the manager and employee should sign the review documentation in **Workday** to confirm the review was conducted and feedback was received. Employee signature does not necessarily indicate agreement with the evaluation.

## 6. Performance Improvement

### 6.1 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
Employees receiving a rating of 1 or consistently receiving ratings of 2 may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan. PIPs include:
- Specific, measurable improvement targets
- Timeline (typically 30-60 days)
- Support and resources provided
- Regular check-ins (weekly minimum)

Failure to meet PIP objectives may result in further disciplinary action, including termination.

### 6.2 Developmental Support
Employees receiving ratings of 2 or 3 should work with their manager to create a development plan, which may include:
- Training courses (available through company learning platform)
- Stretch assignments
- Mentorship pairing
- External coaching (for high-potential employees)

## 7. Compensation Decisions

Performance ratings inform compensation decisions, including:
- Annual merit increases
- Bonus eligibility
- Equity refresh grants
- Promotions

Merit increase guidelines are communicated separately each year and are based on company performance, budget, and individual ratings. A rating of 3 is the minimum expectation for a full merit increase.

## 8. Appeals Process

Employees who believe their performance review contains factual errors or was conducted improperly may:

1. **Discuss with manager:** First attempt to resolve directly with the manager
2. **Escalate to HR:** If unresolved, escalate to People Operations within 10 business days of the review meeting
3. **Formal review:** People Operations will review the process and may adjust ratings if procedural violations occurred

Note: The appeals process does not guarantee a rating change, but ensures the review was conducted according to policy.

## 9. Manager Responsibilities

Managers are responsible for:
- Completing reviews on time
- Providing honest, specific, and actionable feedback
- Documenting ratings with evidence
- Attending calibration sessions
- Conducting review meetings in appropriate settings
- Creating development plans for all direct reports
- Ensuring at least one manager is on-site during performance review quarters for in-person evaluations

Failure to complete reviews on time may impact the manager's own performance review.

## 10. Documentation and Records

All performance review documentation is maintained in **Workday** and retained according to company records retention policy. Performance records are confidential and accessible to:
- The employee
- The employee's manager
- People Operations
- Senior leadership (for compensation and promotion decisions)

Employees may request a copy of their historical performance reviews from People Operations.
